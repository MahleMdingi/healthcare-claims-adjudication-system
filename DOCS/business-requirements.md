# Healthcare Claims Adjudication System

## System Overview

The Healthcare Claims Adjudication System is a medical aid claims processing system designed to automate the validation and adjudication of healthcare claims submitted by medical providers.

The purpose of the system is to assess whether healthcare claims should be approved, partially approved, or rejected based on predefined business rules and medical aid benefit structures.

The system aims to reduce manual claims processing and improve operational efficiency within a medical aid environment.

---

# Business Problem

Medical aid organizations process large volumes of healthcare claims daily.

Manual claims processing can:

* consume significant business resources
* slow down claim turnaround times
* increase operational costs
* increase the risk of human error

The organization requires a centralized claims adjudication system capable of validating claims automatically based on member eligibility, benefit rules, waiting periods, and medical plan coverage.

---

# Main Business Processes

## 1. Claim Submission

Healthcare providers submit claims for medical services rendered to members.

---

## 2. Claim Validation

The system validates:

* member status
* provider information
* medical plan eligibility
* procedure codes
* claim dates

---

## 3. Claims Adjudication

The system determines whether claims should be:

* approved
* partially approved
* rejected

based on business and benefit rules.

---

## 4. Benefit Validation

The system checks:

* available benefits
* annual benefit limits
* plan coverage rules
* waiting periods

---

## 5. Claims Reporting

The system generates operational and analytical reports related to:

* claims activity
* rejected claims
* provider utilization
* member claim trends

---

# Main Actors

## Members

Medical aid members who receive healthcare services and submit claims through providers.

---

## Providers

Healthcare providers such as:

* doctors
* hospitals
* pharmacies
* specialists

who submit healthcare claims to the medical aid system.

---

## Administrators

Internal staff responsible for:

* monitoring claims
* managing plans and benefits
* reviewing claim exceptions
* maintaining system operations

---

# Core Business Rules

Examples of business rules that the system will enforce include:

* members must have active medical aid coverage
* waiting periods must be completed before certain claims are approved
* claims must fall within benefit limits
* tariff/procedure codes must exist and be valid
* duplicate claims should not be processed
* providers must be registered and active
